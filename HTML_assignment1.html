Rat


Description:Rats are small mammals that belong to the rodent family. They are known for their long tails, sharp incisors and pointed snout. There are many different species of rats, but the most common include the brown rat (Rattus norvegicus) and the black rat (Rattus rattus). Brown rats are larger than black rats, and are typically found in urban areas and near human settlements, while black rats are more commonly found in more rural areas. Rats are known to be carriers of diseases and parasites, as well as destructive to property and food supplies.

Gorilla

Description:
  1. Gorillas are a type of primate that are native to the forests of central and eastern Africa.
  2. They are the largest of the living primates, with males weighing up to 400 pounds.
  3. Gorillas are herbivores, primarily eating leaves, stems, fruit, and bamboo.
  4. They live in groups led by a dominant male, called a silverback, who is responsible for the protection of the group.
  5. Gorillas are critically endangered due to habitat loss and poaching.
  6. There are two main species of gorillas: the Eastern Gorilla and Western Gorilla.
